Scope

UX/UI Design Ops Accessibility Mentoring Stakeholder management
The challenge

Context

A growing platform that needed more than just design execution.

Great Minds needed a design team that could operate autonomously, maintain WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards across a complex multi-user platform, and build scalable processes, within a lean, high-trust team structure where designers were expected to own their work end-to-end.

Beyond the product work, the team needed shared knowledge, common tools, and a culture of quality that could survive team changes and account growth.

What I did
1

Autonomous client ownership

Independently managed two full product tracks, Live Mode and Planning, with direct client communication. Represented design decisions, presented solutions, and iterated based on feedback without intermediary. This became my operating model across all Globant accounts.

  • Joined client calls solo, no intermediary between design and client
  • Prepared and led design reviews, presented solutions directly
  • Negotiated scope changes in real time based on business feedback
  • Full ownership of outcome across Live Mode and Planning tracks

Live Mode Reinvention, redesign of the teacher-facing platform, from heuristic evaluation through final proposal. Low adoption triggered a full UX overhaul.

2

Design Ops initiative, end to end

Identified the need, built the plan, assigned tasks, and led the team in creating a comprehensive toolkit covering methods, templates, and resources for every stage of the design process. Presented first at a client Design Showcase, then expanded to Globant's Latin America design studio.

  • Surfaced the pain point: designers reinventing the wheel on every project
  • Templates covered: Discover, Define, Ideate, and Deliver phases, research planning, synthesis, ideation, flows, handoff, and testing
  • Built collaboratively, assigned experts per tool, tested at 2022 design summit
  • Published for free use across the studio, still active today

Methods & Tools site, overview of templates by design phase, including Interview Wizard and Heuristic Evaluation tools.

3

Design Talks, internal & client-facing

Led "Nielsen Is Not Alone", a talk on usability heuristics for the design team, expanding evaluation frameworks beyond Nielsen. Delivered "What and How You Can Test" to the client, demonstrating the team's research and testing capabilities.

  • Nielsen Is Not Alone: covered 8+ heuristic repositories (Web 9, Psychological Usability, IA Heuristics, Laws of UX), revisited at the 2022 design summit
  • What and How You Can Test: explained testing types, metrics, and when to apply each, framed as education, functioned as a pre-sales pitch
  • Months after the testing talk, client expanded engagement to include research and usability testing services
4

Accessibility documentation

Co-designed the a11y behavior template for developers, tab order, aria labels, focus order, alt texts, heading structure, ensuring WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. Promoted accessibility documentation as a standard practice within the team, supported by Deque expert consulting.

  • Integrated into every design flow, documented after VD approval, before dev handoff
  • Covered: tab order, component types, aria labels (Live, Assertive), alt text, focus order at 100/200/400%, heading hierarchy
  • Reduced developer back-and-forth, specs included behavior, not just screens
  • Supported by Deque expert consulting for edge cases and validation
5

"Unlocking Accessibility" book

Initiative Leader and co-author of a practical accessibility guide tailored to educational platforms, covering WCAG standards, reading order, aria labels, interactive zones, color as information, and reflow. Created to onboard designers and developers with clear, applicable principles from day one.

  • Initiative Leader and co-author, designed the book to be accessible itself
  • Topics: WCAG intro, reading order, heading structure, aria labels, alt texts, interactive zones, text length, color as information, reflow and zoom
  • Written for both designers and developers, clear language, visual examples, real platform scenarios
  • Became the first-day onboarding resource for anyone joining the account
6

Mentoring, QC designers transitioning to UX

Mentored QA professionals transitioning into UX within the Great Minds account, helping them build skills and prepare for their UX certification exam.

  • Assigned simple flows for them to work on as hands-on practice within the real project
  • Held regular 1:1s to share resources, clarify UX concepts, and give feedback on their work
  • Trained them on methodology, design principles, and process, preparing them for the formal UX exam
  • Included them in team critique sessions alongside the full design team
